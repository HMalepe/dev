A guest at a private game lodge near Hoedspruit died during what the lodge described, in its own initial incident report, as a routine early-morning walking trail.

Margaret Sinclair, fifty-five, was on the second day of a booked stay, according to the lodge's guest registry submitted to the inquest. She was accompanied on the trail by a single guide, per the lodge's staffing schedule for that morning.

The mechanism examined at inquest was less about the stated cause — a fall — and more about the injury pattern. The forensic pathologist testified that the fracture pattern found was inconsistent with a fall from the height described in the guide's incident statement, and more consistent, in the pathologist's words on record, with "a fall from a significantly greater height or a separate mechanism of blunt trauma."

The unraveling, according to the inquest transcript, came from GPS tracking data on the lodge-issued radio the guide carried, which placed the pair, for a period of roughly twenty minutes, at a location nearly a kilometre from where the guide's statement said the fall occurred.

The inquest returned an open verdict, and the file remains active with provincial investigators, per the most recently published case status.
