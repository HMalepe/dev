A small chemical packaging plant outside Durban suffered a fatal explosion on a Sunday, when the facility should have been empty.

The plant's own occupancy log, cited in the labour department's investigation report, showed no scheduled staff that day. One employee, Bongani Zulu, forty-one, was nonetheless on site, according to the same log's manual sign-in sheet.

The mechanism at the centre of the investigation, per the department's report, was a gas line pressure reading recorded by the plant's automated monitoring system roughly ninety minutes before the explosion — a reading the report describes as "well outside normal operating parameters" and one that, per the system's own alert log, should have triggered an automatic shutdown but did not.

What broke the case open, according to the investigation findings, was a maintenance record showing the automatic shutdown valve had been manually disabled eleven days earlier for a repair that was never logged as completed, despite the work order being marked closed in the plant's system.

The matter has been referred for regulatory and possible criminal review, and the investigation's findings remain the only public record of the case's current status.
