A delivery driver was found dead on a quiet stretch of the N2 outside Mossel Bay at dawn. Investigators initially treated it as a single-vehicle pedestrian accident.

Andries Human, thirty-eight, was walking home along the road's shoulder after his van broke down two kilometres away, according to the breakdown callout log later obtained by investigators. There were no working streetlights on that stretch, per the municipality's own maintenance records.

The mechanism the accident reconstruction report focused on was impact geometry: according to the reconstruction expert's testimony, the injury pattern and the distance the body was found from the point of impact were inconsistent with the speed limit posted on that stretch of road, suggesting a significantly higher speed than the 80 km/h limit.

What broke the case, per the reconstruction report, was paint transfer: a fragment of vehicle trim recovered at the scene was matched, through a national paint database search cited in the report, to a specific vehicle model and production year, narrowing the search considerably.

The case remains open, and the reconstruction report notes that the search for the vehicle is ongoing, per the most recent police statement on file.
