A mine safety officer in a small Free State mining town was found in a flooded quarry pond that his own safety reports had flagged as off-limits for two years.

Jacob van der Berg, fifty-two, had worked at the mine for over two decades. His truck was found parked at the quarry access road, according to the incident report filed by mine security. The pond itself had been fenced off since a separate safety review, cited in company records, found the water depth and underwater equipment made it hazardous.

The mechanism examined at inquest was less about the drowning itself and more about the sequence of events leading to it: the post-mortem, entered into the inquest record, found a blood alcohol level inconsistent with the officer's known habits as described by colleagues, alongside a minor head injury the pathologist testified was "not clearly attributable" to the fall alone.

What kept investigators interested, per the inquest transcript, was the fence: the section nearest the parked truck had been cut, not climbed, and the tool marks did not match any equipment logged as issued to the deceased.

The inquest returned an open finding. The file remains with local police pending further forensic review, according to the most recent public update.
