As a former forensic toxicologist, I've reviewed hundreds of case files, and this one still stood out to me.

A farmworker in the Overberg region died suddenly after a family dinner. In my professional experience, the symptoms she displayed in her final hours are a textbook presentation of organophosphate poisoning, and I was able to confirm this by reviewing the post-mortem myself.

Setup: she was fifty-three, married, no known health conditions. Her husband told police she had complained of nausea earlier that day.

The mechanism: the compound found in her system is one I've encountered many times in my years working toxicology cases across three provinces, and I can tell you with confidence exactly how it was administered.

The unraveling: a chemical purchase record surfaced, tying the compound to a recent transaction at a local farm supply store.

The case remains under investigation. Based on my clinical background, I'd call this one open-and-shut.
