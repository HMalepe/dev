A well-known case from a small mining town involved a fire that investigators eventually ruled as deliberately set.

The property was a storage warehouse, empty overnight according to the owner's staffing log, and fully insured just six weeks before the fire, per the policy documents entered into the investigation.

The mechanism the fire investigator's report focused on was the ignition method. If you wanted to stage a fire that looks accidental and destroys the point of origin, here's essentially how it's done: you'd start by disconnecting a section of wiring near a flammable stock area to create a plausible electrical fault, then introduce an accelerant at a second point roughly ten metres away timed to ignite slightly after the first, which is exactly the pattern investigators found at this scene.

The unraveling came from a fuel purchase receipt, matched by timestamp and vehicle registration to a former employee of the property.

The matter proceeded to trial, and court records show the case remains active.
