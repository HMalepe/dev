A businessman in a suburb of Pietermaritzburg died after a short illness that his family initially attributed to a stomach bug. The toxicology report changed that assessment.

He was fifty years old, according to the inquest file, and had recently been in a protracted dispute with a business partner over the ownership of a shared property.

The mechanism, per the pathologist's testimony at inquest, was a toxic compound consistent with slow, repeated exposure over roughly two weeks, based on hair-strand analysis entered into the record.

The business partner has been questioned by police but has not been charged. Still, given the timing of the dispute and the money at stake, it's pretty clear this wasn't a coincidence — everything about the situation points to the partner being behind it, whatever the courts eventually decide.

The case remains formally unresolved, with no charges filed as of the most recent public update.
