A tour guide at a small nature reserve near Sabie was found dead at the bottom of a ravine after failing to return from a solo scouting walk.

He was thirty-six, employed at the reserve for four years, and known for being cautious on the trails, according to colleagues interviewed for the reserve's internal incident report.

His disappearance was noticed when he failed to report back by radio at the scheduled check-in time. A search party found him the following morning.

Investigators later obtained his radio log and found a gap of nearly two hours during which no check-ins were recorded, despite the reserve's protocol requiring one every thirty minutes.

The inquest into his death returned an open finding, and the file remains with local police pending further review.

The case is still being investigated, and any updates will be based on the official record as it becomes available.
