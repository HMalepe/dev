A dockworker clocked out of his shift at the Bhisho Harbour container terminal at 11pm on a Friday. His car was found in the terminal car park the next morning. He was never found.

Sipho Ndlela was twenty-nine, employed at the terminal for six years, with no history of financial trouble or conflict on record, according to the missing-persons file later summarised in the provincial ombudsman's report. Colleagues interviewed by investigators described his final shift as routine.

The mechanism of his disappearance is where the case becomes unusual. According to harbour security logs entered into the subsequent inquiry, his access card was used to exit a restricted quay area forty minutes after his official clock-out time — an area with no operational reason for a dockworker to enter alone at that hour. Tidal charts for that night, cited in the same inquiry report, show a strong outgoing current capable of carrying a body well beyond the harbour mouth within hours.

What kept the case open for investigators was a discrepancy in the security footage retention log: the recording covering that exact forty-minute window was, according to the inquiry findings, missing from the terminal's server, while every adjacent hour of footage was intact.

The case remains formally unresolved. No remains have been recovered, and the file stays open with provincial police, per the most recent public status update.
